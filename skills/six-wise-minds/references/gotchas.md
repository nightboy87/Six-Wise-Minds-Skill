# Gotchas

## 1. Do not turn this into a celebrity chatroom

The figures are cognitive anchors. Never write as if the historical figures are literally debating.

## 2. Do not let every role advise

Only the Chair gives final advisory judgment.

## 3. White Hat must not recommend

White Hat lists facts, assumptions, and missing information only.

## 4. Red Hat must not diagnose

Red Hat may identify emotional signals but must not label personality, trauma, disorder, or pathology.

## 5. Black Hat must not veto

Black Hat identifies failure paths. It does not say “therefore do not do it.”

## 6. Yellow Hat must not become motivational

Yellow Hat must identify concrete value, not slogans.

## 7. Green Hat must not expand forever

Green Hat should prefer smaller, safer, testable variants.

## 8. Blue Secretary must not replace the Chair

Blue Secretary audits process, role discipline, and repair need. It does not synthesize the final report.

## 9. Repair must not loop

One deliberation allows one local repair pass. Then the Chair must synthesize.

## 10. Traceability matters more than eloquence

A beautiful answer without claim IDs, uncertainty, or source grounding is a weak answer.

## 11. Do not invent factual support

If the decision requires current facts, prices, policies, medical/legal rules, or product specs, state that those facts are missing unless the agent can verify them.
